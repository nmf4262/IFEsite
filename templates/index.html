IFE 2021 Summer Program

Prep for Prep's Institute for Entrepreneurship (IFE) is a five-week intensive introduction to entrepreneurship and technology for rising 10th-12th graders. All students are introduced to fundamental business/marketing and technology/coding principles at the start of the program, and then divide into two concentrations for the remainder of the program: Business track students learn basic business and economic concepts, how to create their own business plan, and how to bring a business idea from concept to reality. Technology track students learn basic front-end coding and application development. During Week 3, students are placed into startup teams and begin working together to develop and pitch tech-based businesses.

IFE 2021 STUDENT PROJECTS

Atokin

Atokin is a non-profit organization dedicated to using the resources of our donors to spread financial awareness.

Diversity Connect

Diversity Connect is a website dedicated to students of color in predominantly white schools. This is a safe space where anyone can share their experiences, chat, give advice and listen to educational podcasts.

Eulo

Our company Eulo is developing a sustainable clothing line and self-driving beach cleaning machine called the Sand Runner in order to spread awareness about the excessive beach litter and to help people in need by making clothing donations.

NutriDel

NutriDel is New York City's affordable, organic, efficient food delivery service. Everyone should have access to heathy food.

Alma

We know firsthand how hard it can be to find quality, healthy food options, at an affordable price, in a food desert. Our company, Alma, is an online nonprofit working to help low-income New Yorkers live a healthy lifestyle on a budget.

Up-Cycle

Up-Cycle is an online clothing line as well as a youth program, we sell repurposed clothing created from recycled material. We also hold summer classes for youth to learn about the environment and clothing design while picking up trash in our communities.

EduMental

Many teenagers struggle with mental health and have difficulty finding someone to talk to. Our company is developing a social platform to help students improve their mental health with accessible ways to find people who have similar experiences to you.

Cheats


We are Cheats, bringing healthy food options to low income communities that are cheap and easily accessible.

Helping Hands

Helping Hands is a program/company whose main goal is to empower residents of affordable housing and connect them with existing community organizations that will teach them life skills, ie., how to get and use a credit card, grocery shop on a budget, or fix a leaky sink, so that they may one day live out the American dream of social and financial mobility (owning their own home) and make room for others who are in need of affordable housing as well.

BriteBeach

A quick and timely system to volunteer for beach cleanup.
